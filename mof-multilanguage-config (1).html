Translation Quality Guide
Review each AI translation for:

1. Medical accuracy — terms like CPAP, apnea, polysomnography must be exact

2. Patient-friendly tone — avoid overly clinical language in patient-facing scripts

3. Cultural appropriateness — honorifics, formality level, and idioms vary by culture

4. Emergency scripts — 911 instructions must be verified by bilingual medical staff
Emergency scripts (item #12) require verification by bilingual medical staff before deployment. MOF will flag these for Technical Admin review.

Voice Tips
For Spanish: Latin American accent (ES-419) is preferred over Castilian (ES-ES) for US-based practices with Mexican/Central American patient populations.

For Mandarin: If your patient base is primarily from Taiwan or Hong Kong, consider Mei Ling (ZH-TW) over Lin Mei (ZH-CN). The written characters also differ.

Twilio Unicode Support
Twilio supports full Unicode in SMS, including:

• Chinese characters (Simplified + Traditional)
• Spanish accented characters (á, é, ñ)
• Emoji (✓ 🩺 🏥)

Note: Unicode SMS uses 70 chars per segment vs 160 for ASCII. Long Mandarin messages may split into 2 segments ($0.015 × 2).
Keep Mandarin SMS under 70 characters to avoid splitting into 2 message segments. Current template: 62 chars ✓
