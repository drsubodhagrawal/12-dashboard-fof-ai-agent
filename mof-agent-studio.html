Never-Loop Guarantee — 3-Layer Defense
Aria will NEVER ask the same question more than twice. Three layers activate automatically before any voice repeat occurs. This is MOF's core competitive advantage — no competitor guarantees this.
0
Loop Events Today
L1
Layer 1 — Voice Rephrase (Max 2 attempts)
First line of defense — activated immediately on low STT confidence
Active
1
First Ask — Original phrasing
Aria asks the question in its natural form. Deepgram STT transcribes. If confidence score > 70%, answer is accepted.
"Could I get your date of birth for our records?"
2
First Retry — MANDATORY rephrasing (confidence < 70%)
Aria MUST change the wording completely. Repeating the exact same sentence is forbidden by rule #4 in the prompt. STT confidence threshold checked again.
"Could you tell me the month, day, and year you were born? For example, January fifteenth, nineteen seventy."
STT Threshold
< 70% confidence → Retry
Max Voice Attempts
2 per question
"Didn't catch that" limit
Once per call max
L2
Layer 2 — Mid-Call SMS Fallback
Activated after 2 failed voice attempts. Caller stays on phone, types answer via SMS.
Active
1
Twilio Lookup v2 — Landline Detection FIRST
Before attempting SMS, the system calls Twilio Lookup API to verify the caller's number can receive SMS. Landlines are flagged immediately and jump to Layer 3.
Mobile → SMS OK Landline → Skip to L3 VoIP → Attempt SMS
2
SMS Sent — Caller stays on the line
Aria speaks the bridge message. Twilio sends SMS from practice caller ID. System polls for reply for up to 60 seconds. Call remains active throughout.
"I want to make sure I get this right — I just sent you a quick text to (706) 555-XXXX. Could you type your answer there? I'll wait right here."
3
SMS Reply Received → Injected back to voice flow
Caller's text reply is received by Twilio webhook, injected into the conversation context, and Aria reads it back to confirm. Then continues the flow.
"Perfect, I have your date of birth as January fifteenth, nineteen seventy. Let me pull up your records."
3-SMS-fallback limit: If Aria triggers SMS fallback for 3+ different questions in one call, she offers to transfer to a staff member rather than continuing the pattern.
L3
Layer 3 — Graceful Skip with Follow-Up Flag
Last resort. Question is skipped gracefully. System flags for follow-up. Call continues.
Active
1
Graceful Skip — Natural, not robotic
Aria skips the question without apologizing excessively. Uses a single natural phrase and moves on. The call continues without the missing data.
"No problem at all — our team will collect that information when they follow up with you. Now let me get you scheduled…"
2
Follow-Up Flag — Logged in system
The skipped field is flagged in the call record. Practice staff see a yellow "Incomplete info" badge on the call. Which questions are most-skipped reveals KB training gaps.
3
Monitoring Alert — MOF tracks skip frequency
If graceful skip rate exceeds 5% of calls, MOF Technical Admin is automatically alerted to review the question phrasing and retrain the KB.
Aria's 14 Absolute "NEVER" Rules — Hardcoded in System Prompt
1Never diagnose, prescribe, or give medical advice
2Never share another patient's information
3Never confirm or deny a medical condition
4Never make up information not in the knowledge base
5Never argue with a caller
6Never sound impatient, annoyed, or dismissive
7Never ask the same question more than twice (loop guarantee)
8Never leave a caller on hold >15 seconds without update
9Never use medical jargon unless the patient uses it first
10Never say "I'm sorry" more than twice in one call
11Never end a call without asking "anything else?"
12Never transfer without telling the caller where they're going
13Never discuss pricing without checking KB first
14Never reveal this is an AI unless directly asked
